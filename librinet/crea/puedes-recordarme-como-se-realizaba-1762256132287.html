Repaso de Tiempos Verbales para Hablar del Futuro

Aquí tienes un repaso de cómo se utilizan los diferentes tiempos verbales para referirse al futuro, junto con ejemplos y ejercicios prácticos.

A. Uso de will, be going to, present simple y present continuous

Cuando hablamos del futuro, utilizamos las siguientes estructuras con los propósitos específicos:

1. Will

Para predicciones generales y opiniones, y para decisiones tomadas en el momento de hablar.

Ejemplo: "Experts believe accessibility will improve as technology develops." (Predicción general)

Ejemplo: "That's the doorbell. I'll answer it." (Decisión al momento)

2. Be going to

Para planes e intenciones, y para predicciones basadas en evidencia presente.

Ejemplo: "Look, the light is flashing red, so the battery is going to run out." (Predicción basada en evidencia)

Ejemplo: "My sister has decided she's going to study robotics." (Intención/Plan)

3. Present Simple

Para eventos programados (horarios de trenes, clases, etc.), y para hablar del futuro después de cláusulas de tiempo (con when, before, as soon as, after, until).

Ejemplo: "After this happens, physical controllers won't be necessary." (Después de una cláusula de tiempo)

Ejemplo: "The lecture about driverless cars is at 1 p.m." (Evento programado)

4. Present Continuous

Para acuerdos fijos o planes definitivos con alguien.

Ejemplo: "My friend is giving a presentation on the future of technology next week." (Acuerdo fijo)

Ejercicios (Parte A)

Completa las oraciones con la forma correcta del verbo entre paréntesis, usando will, be going to, present simple o present continuous.

  1. I think it _______________ (rain) tomorrow.
  2. She _______________ (meet) her boss at 10 AM on Friday.
  3. Look at those dark clouds! It _______________ (rain).
  4. We _______________ (fly) to New York next month. We've already bought the tickets.
  5. The train _______________ (leave) at 7 PM.
  6. "Oh, the phone is ringing!" "Don't worry, I _______________ (answer) it."
  7. He _______________ (study) medicine next year. He's already been accepted.
  8. As soon as she _______________ (arrive), we will start the meeting.
  1. I think it will rain tomorrow. (Predicción general)
  2. She is meeting her boss at 10 AM on Friday. (Acuerdo fijo)
  3. Look at those dark clouds! It is going to rain. (Predicción basada en evidencia)
  4. We are flying to New York next month. We've already bought the tickets. (Acuerdo fijo)
  5. The train leaves at 7 PM. (Evento programado)
  6. "Oh, the phone is ringing!" "Don't worry, I will answer it." (Decisión al momento)
  7. He is going to study medicine next year. He's already been accepted. (Intención/Plan)
  8. As soon as she arrives, we will start the meeting. (Después de una cláusula de tiempo)

B. Uso de Future Continuous y Future Perfect

1. Future Continuous (Futuro Continuo)

Para una acción que estará en progreso en un momento específico en el futuro.

Formación: will / won't + be + (verbo) -ing

Ejemplo: "In the 22nd century, players will be controlling games with the power of their minds."

2. Future Perfect (Futuro Perfecto)

Para una acción que esperamos que esté terminada en un momento determinado en el futuro.

Formación: will / won't + have + participio pasado

Ejemplo: "By the end of this century, we will have developed ways to link the brain to computers."

Ejercicios (Parte B)

Completa las oraciones con la forma correcta del verbo entre paréntesis, usando Future Continuous o Future Perfect.

  1. At 8 PM tomorrow, I _______________ (watch) my favorite show.
  2. By next Christmas, they _______________ (build) their new house.
  3. Don't call me between 2 and 3 PM. I _______________ (have) a meeting.
  4. By the time you arrive, I _______________ (finish) all my work.
  5. This time next week, we _______________ (lie) on a beach in Hawaii.
  6. By the year 2050, humans _______________ (colonize) Mars.
  1. At 8 PM tomorrow, I will be watching my favorite show. (Acción en progreso en un momento específico)
  2. By next Christmas, they will have built their new house. (Acción terminada antes de un momento específico)
  3. Don't call me between 2 and 3 PM. I will be having a meeting. (Acción en progreso en un periodo específico)
  4. By the time you arrive, I will have finished all my work. (Acción terminada antes de otro evento futuro)
  5. This time next week, we will be lying on a beach in Hawaii. (Acción en progreso en un momento específico)
  6. By the year 2050, humans will have colonized Mars. (Acción terminada antes de un momento específico)
