Guía para Escribir una Narrativa en Inglés

Objetivo: Escribir una Narrativa

Una narrativa es un texto que describe una experiencia, real o imaginaria. Su propósito es contar una historia de una manera atractiva y vívida.

Ejemplo de Narración: An experience I'll never forget

Last summer, **while** I was staying at a campsite with my family in the Rocky Mountains in Canada, I had an experience I'll never forget. In fact, in the middle of the night, my parents had been asleep in our tent for a few hours, **when** all of a sudden, a strange noise woke me up. It was a kind of heavy sniffing sound. **At first**, I thought it was my dad snoring, but then I realized it was coming from outside the tent and it was moving around. **Then**, I saw the shadow of a bear in the moonlight. I was absolutely terrified and chose to lie still, pretending to be asleep, but my parents weren't asleep. **Shortly after that**, just then, I pulled my sleeping bag over my head and kept completely still. **After a few minutes**, the noise got fainter and more distant, and we realized that the bear had gone away. What a relief! **Eventually**, we managed to get back to sleep. In the morning, we went to get our breakfast, which was in the car. Imagine our surprise when we discovered that the bear had opened the car door and stolen all the bread and cereal! Although it was very scary at the time, it hasn't put me off camping in bear country. However, in the future, I'll definitely think twice about leaving any food in the car, and definitely not in the tent!

Más Ejemplos de Narrativas

A Disastrous Camping Trip

Last spring, my friends and I decided to go camping in the local national park. We were so excited to spend a weekend surrounded by nature. We set up our tents by a beautiful river, full of enthusiasm. **Initially**, everything was perfect; we cooked a delicious meal over the campfire and told ghost stories. **However**, during the night, a sudden thunderstorm hit. The rain was **torrential**, and the wind was **fierce**. **First**, our tent started leaking, and **then**, a strong gust of wind ripped one of the poles. We were **terrified** and soaking wet. **Eventually**, we had to pack up in the pouring rain and seek refuge in the car. It was a **disastrous** night, but **even now**, we laugh about our soggy adventure. It definitely taught us to check the weather forecast more carefully!

The Unexpected Gift

One cold winter morning, as I was rushing to catch my train, I slipped on a patch of ice and fell, scattering my books and papers across the frosty pavement. I felt incredibly **embarrassed** and a little **shaken**, especially since my glasses had flown off. **At first**, I just lay there, a bit stunned, trying to gather my thoughts. People hurried past, oblivious or perhaps just unwilling to stop. **Then**, a kind elderly woman, who was walking her small dog, stopped beside me. **Without hesitation**, she knelt down, gathering my scattered belongings with a gentle smile. She even found my glasses and carefully placed them back on my face. **Eventually**, with her help, I managed to stand up, brushing off the snow. I thanked her **profusely**, feeling a surge of **gratitude**. **Even now**, I remember her warm smile and generous spirit. Her small act of kindness truly made my day, reminding me that compassion still exists in the busiest of cities.

Proceso de Escritura de una Narrativa

1. Conseguir ideas (Get ideas)

  • Lee la tarea cuidadosamente e identifica qué tipo de texto necesitas escribir.
  • Haz notas de cualquier idea clave. No escribas oraciones completas.
  • Piensa en una experiencia memorable (positiva o negativa) que hayas tenido o que podrías inventar.
  • Haz notas sobre:
    • ¿Cuándo y dónde sucedió?
    • ¿Quiénes estaban allí?
    • ¿Qué sucedió primero, después, finalmente?
    • ¿Cómo te sentiste?
    • ¿Cómo te sientes ahora?
  • Piensa en adjetivos para describir cómo te sentiste y qué pensaste.

2. Planificar (Plan)

  • Elige las mejores ideas de tus notas.
  • Piensa en el lenguaje útil para tu narrativa.
  • Organiza tus ideas en párrafos.
  • Un plan de ejemplo:
    • Párrafo 1: Introducción - Presenta la historia y explica el trasfondo.
    • Párrafos 2 y 3: Describe la parte principal de la historia.
    • Párrafo 4: Describe cómo te sentiste y cómo te afectó la experiencia.

3. Escribir (Write)

  • Utiliza tu plan de párrafos como guía.
  • Usa conectores para organizar la secuencia de eventos (ver sección de "Vocabulario").
  • Utiliza una variedad de tiempos verbales.
  • Haz tu historia más interesante usando sonidos, vistas y movimiento.
  • Incluye algunas frases útiles (ver sección de "Vocabulario").

4. Revisar (Check)

  • Lee tu narrativa cuidadosamente.
  • ¿Has usado los tiempos pasados correctamente?
  • ¿Has usado las palabras de tiempo correctamente?
  • ¿Has usado una variedad de adjetivos y adverbios para describir emociones, sonidos, vistas y movimiento?
  • ¿Has incluido algunas de las frases útiles?
  • ¿Se ha respondido completamente a la tarea?
  • ¿Has usado el número correcto de palabras?
  • ¿Tu composición tiene una organización clara?
  • ¿Has evitado la repetición siempre que sea posible?
  • ¿Has revisado la puntuación, la ortografía y la gramática?

Vocabulario y Frases Útiles

Frases Útiles para una Narrativa

  • I had an experience I'll never forget...
  • I clearly remember...
  • All of a sudden...
  • At first, I thought... but then I realized...
  • Imagine my / our / his / her / their surprise / astonishment / relief when...
  • What a relief!
  • Even now...
  • I've never forgotten...
  • It was in fact / actually...

Usando Conectores (Linkers) para ordenar eventos cronológicamente

Initially In begin with (...) In the end Shortly after that just then At first After a while Finally Then when Eventually after all of a sudden to begin with To show events happening at the same time: as, (...) To end a sequence of events: In the end, (...)

Frases para Añadir Ideas

  • Para empezar una oración: Also, ... As well as ... For example: ... Moreover, ... Furthermore, ... In addition to (this), ... What's more.
  • Para unir palabras/cláusulas: ... and ... ; ... as well as ... ; ... What's more.
  • Para terminar una oración o cláusula: ... too. ; ... as well.

Frases para Contrastar Ideas

  • Para empezar una oración: One (dis)advantage is ... ; Another (dis)advantage is (that) ... ; The main argument for / against is ... ; However, ... ; Nevertheless, ... ; Nonetheless, ... ; On the one / other hand, ... ; Although / Even though ... ; Despite / In spite of the fact that ...
  • Para unir palabras/cláusulas: ... but ... ; ... yet ... ; ... although / even though / despite / in spite of ...
  • Para terminar una oración o cláusula: ... though.

Frases para Expresar Razón

  • Para empezar una oración: Because ... ; Since ... ; As ... ; That is why ... ; Due to / Because of (noun) ... ; For this / that reason.
  • Para unir cláusulas: ... because / since / as ... ; ... due to / because of (+ noun) / because (+ clause) ...
  • Para terminar una oración o cláusula: ... for that reason.

Frases para Expresar Resultado

  • Para empezar una oración: Therefore, ... ; As a result, ... ; As a consequence, ... ; Consequently.
  • Para unir cláusulas: ... so ...
  • Para terminar una oración o cláusula: ... as a consequence. ; ... as a result.

Frases para Expresar Razón y Resultado

  • ... so (+ adjective) that ...
  • ... such (a/an) (+ adjective) noun that ...

Frases para Expresar Propósito

  • Para empezar una oración: In order that / to (+ infinitive).
  • Para unir palabras/cláusulas: ... in order that ... ; ... so (that) ... ; ... in order to / to (+ infinitive).
  • Para terminar una oración o cláusula: ... for that purpose.

Frases para Hacer Condiciones

  • Para empezar una oración: If ... ; As long as ... ; Providing that ... ; Unless (clause), (clause) ...
  • Para unir cláusulas: ... if ... ; ... as long as ... ; ... providing that ... ; ... unless ...

Frases para Secuenciar un Argumento o Narrativa

  • Para empezar una oración: Firstly, ... ; First of all, ... ; Secondly, ... ; Next, ... ; Thirdly, ... ; Finally, ... ; Lastly.
  • Para empezar una sentencia: Before (+ clause / noun -ing) ... ; At first, ... ; To begin with, ... ; Then ... ; Next, ... ; After that, ... ; In the end, ... ; Eventually, ... ; Finally, ... ; Afterwards, ... ; After (+ clause / noun -ing).
  • Para unir cláusulas: ... before ... ; ... after ... ; ... then ...
  • Para terminar una oración o cláusula: ... in the end. ; ... afterwards. ; ... after all. ; ... at last.
